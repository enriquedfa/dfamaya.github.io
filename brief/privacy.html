Privacy Policy — Brief

Last updated: 27 June 2026

Brief is a Wear OS “at a glance” app: a watch complication and tile that show the single most relevant thing right now — your now-playing music, your next calendar event, the current weather, a scheduled reminder, or today’s date. It comes in two parts: the watch app (which only displays what it’s told) and the phone companion (which gathers the information and sends it to your watch).

This policy explains exactly what Brief accesses, why, and where it goes. The short version: Brief has no servers, no account, no analytics, and no ads. Almost everything stays on your own devices. The only thing that ever leaves your phone is an approximate location, sent to a weather provider so it can return your local forecast.

Who is responsible

Brief is an independent app. The developer is the “data controller” for the purposes of this policy. Questions or requests: developerdfa@gmail.com.

What Brief accesses, and why

Data Where it’s read Why Permission
Calendar events (title, time, location) Your phone, read-only To show your next event on the watch READ_CALENDAR
Approximate location Your phone To fetch your local weather forecast ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION, ACCESS_BACKGROUND_LOCATION
Active media session (track title, artist, play/pause state, app) Your phone To show what’s playing and let you play/pause from the watch Notification access (BIND_NOTIFICATION_LISTENER_SERVICE)
Reminders you create (text, time, icon) Authored by you in the app To show your own scheduled nudges —
Your settings (priority order, units, masking, etc.) Authored by you in the app To configure what shows and how —

Brief requests notification access only to read the active media session (so it can show now-playing and forward play/pause). It does not read, store, or transmit the content of your other notifications.

What leaves your device

  • Approximate location → weather provider. When weather is enabled, Brief sends coordinates to Open-Meteo (open-meteo.com) over an encrypted (HTTPS) connection to retrieve the forecast. Open-Meteo requires no account or API key. See Open-Meteo’s own privacy terms at https://open-meteo.com/en/terms. If you set a manual location instead of using your phone’s location, only that manual location is sent. You can disable weather entirely.
  • A location search you type (when choosing a manual location) is sent to Open-Meteo’s geocoding service to turn the place name into coordinates.

Nothing else is sent off your devices. There is no Brief server — calendar titles, media details, reminders, and settings are never uploaded to the developer or any third party.

Between your phone and your watch

Brief’s phone companion sends the resolved glance, your settings, and media play/pause commands to your paired watch over the Wear OS Data Layer (provided by Google Play Services). This travels directly between your own paired devices over their encrypted Bluetooth/Wi-Fi connection. It is not routed through a Brief server.

If you turn on “mask titles,” event titles and locations are replaced with a generic placeholder before they ever leave the phone, so the real text is never sent to the watch.

Where data is stored, and for how long

  • All data Brief keeps lives on-device in private app storage (Android DataStore): the last resolved glance, a cached weather forecast, and your settings.
  • The cached weather forecast and now-playing details expire automatically (e.g. stale weather and paused/old media are dropped within minutes to hours).
  • This private storage is excluded from Android cloud backup and device-to-device transfer, so your weather coordinates and the keywords you choose to hide are not copied off the device by the system backup.
  • Uninstalling Brief removes all of this on-device data.

Data sharing and sale

Brief does not sell your data, share it for advertising, or use it for any purpose other than showing your glance. There are no third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs in the app. The only third party that receives anything is the weather provider (Open-Meteo), and only the approximate location needed to return a forecast.

Permissions, in plain terms

  • Calendar (read-only): read your upcoming events to show the next one. Brief never creates, edits, or deletes your events.
  • Location (approximate, including in the background): fetch local weather. Background access lets the forecast refresh while the app isn’t open; you can avoid it by setting a manual location or disabling weather.
  • Notification access: read the active media session for now-playing and transport controls.
  • Exact alarms / ignore battery optimizations / run at startup: wake the companion at the right moments (e.g. when an event is about to start) so the glance is timely, and restore scheduling after a reboot.

You can revoke any of these in Android Settings at any time; Brief degrades gracefully (the affected source simply stops appearing).

Children

Brief is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from children.

Security

Data stays in private, app-scoped on-device storage; network requests use HTTPS; and the phone↔watch link relies on the platform’s encrypted, pairing-authenticated channel.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the “Last updated” date above will change and the revised policy will be posted at the same location.
